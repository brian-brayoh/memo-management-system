Privacy Policy

At the Office of the Director of Public Prosecutions (ODPP), we respect and value your privacy. This Privacy Policy outlines how we handle, collect, and use personal data to protect your privacy rights.

1. Information Collection

We collect information to improve our services, including data you provide directly and data collected automatically when you interact with our systems.

  • Direct Information: Personal details you provide, such as when you contact us or use our services.
  • Automatic Information: Information collected via cookies, usage logs, and analytics tools, including your IP address, browser type, and visit duration.

2. Use of Information

Your information is used to:

  • Deliver, improve, and customize our services.
  • Respond to your inquiries and provide customer support.
  • Monitor, analyze, and enhance our websiteâ€™s performance.

3. Information Sharing and Disclosure

We limit data sharing to ensure your privacy, only sharing information in these cases:

  • With Consent: When you have provided explicit consent.
  • Legal Compliance: If required by law or legal proceedings.
  • Protection: To protect the rights, security, or integrity of ODPP or the public.

4. Data Security

We use industry-standard security measures to protect your data. However, no digital transmission or storage system is entirely secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

5. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy periodically to reflect changes in our practices. We encourage you to review this page regularly to stay informed of any changes.

6. Contact Us

If you have any questions about this Privacy Policy, please contact us at contact@odpp.gov.ke.
